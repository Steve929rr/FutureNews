Global Carbon Vault Surpasses 1 Trillion Tons Sequestered

In a landmark moment for planetary restoration, the UN-backed Global Carbon Vault announced it has surpassed 1 trillion tons of atmospheric carbon sequestered, nearly offsetting the industrial emissions of the 20th and 21st centuries. Powered by continent-scale direct air capture arrays and next-gen basalt mineralization techniques, the Vault operates across five mega-habitats including the Sahara, Australian Outback, and Martian lowlands. “This marks the beginning of Earth’s rewilding century,” said Ava N’Doye, Secretary-General of the Earth System Stewardship Agency.

Where the Buffalo Roam once again

The successful reintroduction of bison to the Great Plains marks a historic milestone in North America’s ecological restoration efforts. Once on the brink of extinction, these majestic animals now roam freely across vast grasslands, playing a vital role in restoring the natural balance of the ecosystem. The small herds that survived throughout the 20th and 21st centuries in places like Great Plains National Park and Caprock Canyons State Park are now the ancestors of expansive herds stretching across the plains. These thriving populations help maintain healthy grasslands through their grazing patterns. To protect these iconic creatures and ensure sustainable growth, new hunting regulations have been established. Beyond reviving bison populations, this restoration effort has also revitalized local ecosystems and economies, while offering alternatives to the environmental challenges posed by factory farming.
